About Me

I am a full stack web developer with a background in financial analysis. I am currently working as a teacher's assistant and tutor for a web development bootcamp at UC Berkeley. I am a creative, organized candidate, with expertise in web design, development, and high-performance technology driven solutions. I have demonstrated success in problem solving and a proven track record with strong attention to detail. I integrate new information quickly and I feel most engaged when I am in a little over my head or learning something new.

My Story

In school, I always loved math. Something about how numbers worked together tickled a part of my brain and always left me wanting more. This love of numbers led me to take a number of statistics classes in college and eventually major in Environmental Economics. Unfortunately, the world of finance was not what I had imagined it would be. The culture, the people, the actual work I found myself doing all didnâ€™t align with what I really wanted from a career.

I wanted a challenge. I wanted creativity. I wanted to build things and feel excited about my work. I spent some time soul searching and ended up taking some JavaScript classes online. There it was again! That tingly feeling in my brain. Solving algorithms, building applications and websites, finding bugs and fixing them all gave me a rush. Programming took my love of math and brought it to a whole new level. Now I could use that passion to actually be creative or solve problems. I decided to enroll in the Hack Reactor bootcamp and following that I participated in a year long web development apprenticeship program at Learners Guild. Now I am seeking a career in software engineering.
